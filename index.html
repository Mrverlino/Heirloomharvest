Founder's profile

Medefo Talom Sandrine is a strategic agripreneur deeply committed to sustainable agriculture and agribusiness, with a focus on rural development and the mission of zero hunger.
As the founder and Managing Director of Heirloom Harvest, she has a proven track record in modern farming techniques, agribusiness management, and value chain development.

Medefo operates a nursery that supplies a diverse range of seeds and seedlings, including Calina dwarf papaya, mint, stevia, Irish potatoes, and bell peppers.
She integrates her fisheries operations with her nursery to create a circular economy, effectively eliminating waste and promoting sustainability.
Her innovative “ZERO WASTE” model empowers farmers, transforming agriculture into viable business opportunities while enhancing food security.
Medefo’s dedication to healthy living and environmental conservation drives impactful solutions for community development, making her a leader in the agribusiness sector.

A-Fish farming Technelogy

B - Paw Paw Seedlings

Our Calina Dwarf papaya seedlings are carefully raised to ensure high survival rates, early fruiting, and strong resistance to pests and diseases. Perfect for smallholder farmers, backyard growers, and commercial plantations.

Characteristics

1. Compact Growth Habit : Grows to about 3 to 4 feet, ideal for small spaces.

2. Vigorous Growth: Exhibits strong, sturdy stems and excellent fruit-setting ability.

3. Early Maturity: Fruits mature approximately 8 months after planting.

4. Fruit Quality: Average weight: 1.7 to 2.3 kg, thick flesh with an attractive red color, very sweet flavor.

5. Disease Resistance: Good level of virus resistance, contributing to plant health.

6. Growing Conditions: Prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight; requires regular watering.

7. Excellent Fruit Setting Ability: Strong ability to produce fruit efficiently.

8. Strong Thick Stems: Ensures stability and support for fruit production.

9. Nutritional Benefits: Rich in vitamins A, C, and E, as well as dietary fiber.

10. Pollination: Can be male, female, or hermaphroditic; hermaphroditic plants are ideal for self-pollination.
